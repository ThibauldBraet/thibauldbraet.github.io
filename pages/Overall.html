--- layout: page title: NYC From Above subtitle: An Overall Look at New York --- To find the perfect place to live in NYC, it is necessary to consider different metrics. In the other pages of this website, we specifically examine geographical patterns and trends for crime, housing, and education. From those individual analyses, one can get a sense of what places may be ideal to live; however, viewing these trends together is a slightly more complicated case. On this page, we provide a tool that allows for users to input different weights for these factors. This allows for greater flexibility for recommendations; for example, an investment banker without family, money, time or even a life could just be looking for a place to sleep while a family of four attaches a lot of importance to their living conditions. Thatâ€™s why we made a tool to find the best place to live, tailored to your needs.

There are three sliders and a map displayed below. For the sliders, moving the sliders to the right places greater importance on the quality of these; for example, moving crime to the right would improve the score for zip codes that have low crime rates. The housing slider follows a similar pattern, but it is slightly more complicated; the further you move to the right, the higher you value affordability of a house or apartment in that location. Below the map there is also a table with the top ten "best" zip codes. The scores for all are originally constructed from linear combination of the individual metrics, and are then mapped to the range [0, 1].
